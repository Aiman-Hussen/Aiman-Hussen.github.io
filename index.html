About Me

I am a Computer Science student currently based in Berlin, building a multi-disciplinary background that connects software development with real-world technical operations. Before moving into full-time computing, I gained valuable hands-on experience working as a mechanic, which gave me an intuitive understanding of complex physical machines, logistics, and troubleshooting technical systems under pressure. Additionally, my background working in the hotel hospitality sector helped me develop strong skills in workflow organization, team communication, and process management.

Today, I apply this practical logic to software engineering. My current focus centers around core computer science principles, interactive system architecture, front-end development, and game engineering using indie tools like Godot. I treat programming as a tool to bridge the physical and digital worlds—whether configuring backend automation or designing unique interfaces.
